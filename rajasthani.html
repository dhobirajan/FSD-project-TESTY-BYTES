RAJASTHANI :

*Mava Kachori:

Another form of Kachori in Jodhpur is the Mawa Kachori, invented by the late Rawat Deora. It is a sweet dish dipped in sugar syrup. In Rajasthan, it is usually a round ball made of flour and dough filled with a stuffing of yellow moong dal, black pepper, red chili powder, and ginger paste.

*Dal Bhati:

Daal Baati is an Indian dish of daal (lentils) and baati (hard wheat rolls). It is popular in Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh (especially in Braj, Nimar and Malwa regions), Maharashtra's Khandesh and Vidarbha region, Gujarat, and Uttar Pradesh.

*Rajasthani Thal:

Rajasthani Thali is a platter of various dishes that are served together on one plate. It is a complete meal that includes a variety of dishes like dal, bati, churma, kadhi, gatte ki sabzi, papad, and more. The thali is usually served on a large plate, and the dishes are served in small bowls.
