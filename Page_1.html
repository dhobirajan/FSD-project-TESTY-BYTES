PUNJABI ITEMS :

*Makke ki roti & sarso ka sag :

Makki ki roti is a flat unleavened bread made from corn meal (maize flour),[2] primarily eaten in the Jammu region, Himachal Pradesh, Punjab, Haryana, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, and Uttarakhand in North India and Gujarat, Maharashtra in Western India and also in Nepal.[3] Like most rotis in the Indian subcontinent, it is baked on a tava.

*Panjabi thali:

You Can Do It: Punjabi Thali! It is a traditional Indian platter that brings together a medley of authentic Punjabi dishes, showcasing the essence of Punjabi gastronomy. A Punjabi Thali typically consists of an assortment of curries, dal (lentils), bread, rice, pickles, and desserts, all carefully curated to provide a wholesome and balanced meal

*Dal Makhani:

Dal makhani is a dish originating in Punjab region. A relatively modern variation of traditional lentil dishes, it is made with urad dal (black lentils) and other pulses, and includes butter and cream (makhani is a Punjabi word for butter).
